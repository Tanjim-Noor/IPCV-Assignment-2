# Modes and Intake

## Mode routing

Choose the narrowest mode that satisfies the request. Switch modes only when the user changes the objective or the next mode is a necessary completion step.

| Mode | Minimum input | Stop after |
|---|---|---|
| `plan` | Topic or draft objective | Approved brief and plan |
| `outline` | Question and intended contribution | Evidence-mapped outline |
| `draft` | Outline or bounded section goal plus evidence | Requested draft |
| `literature-review` | Review question and scope | Search/synthesis package |
| `abstract` | Paper content or verified summary | Abstract and keywords |
| `grant` | Opportunity or proposal objective | Proposal package |
| `revise` | Draft plus goals or feedback | Applied changes and ledger |
| `reviewer-response` | Reviewer comments; draft optional | Response plan/letter or audit |
| `citation-audit` | Draft and reference material | Audit report |
| `format` | Stable content and target format | Formatted artifact |
| `submission` | Stable manuscript and venue | Checked submission package |
| `full` | Sufficient project brief | All relevant phases |

## Writing Brief

Record:

1. Deliverable and stage.
2. Central question or objective.
3. Intended contribution.
4. Discipline and subfield.
5. Audience and target venue.
6. Language and regional variety.
7. Citation style.
8. Output format.
9. Word/page limit and deadline.
10. Supplied sources, data, drafts, figures, reviews, and templates.
11. Evidence status and missing material.
12. Ethical, confidentiality, authorship, funder, or disclosure constraints.
13. Desired collaboration style: direct drafting, guided planning, coaching, or audit.
14. Decisions requiring approval.

Infer low-risk details from the artifacts. Ask concise questions only for decisions that materially change the result.

## Evidence intake

Classify each input:

- `primary-evidence`: data, protocol, direct source, official policy.
- `secondary-evidence`: review, synthesis, commentary.
- `author-material`: notes, claims, draft prose, interpretations.
- `instruction`: venue template, rubric, reviewer request.
- `unverified-lead`: citation, URL, quotation, or fact not yet checked.
- `placeholder`: intentionally incomplete content.

Do not silently promote author material or an unverified lead to verified evidence.

## Mode-specific gates

- Abstract: require the paper or a sufficiently complete verified summary; do not invent findings.
- Literature review: define databases/sources, dates, query logic, screening rules, and synthesis method.
- Grant: distinguish planned work from completed work and preliminary evidence.
- Revision: identify the canonical draft and preserve untouched content.
- Reviewer response: account for every comment and verify claimed manuscript changes.
- Citation audit: require access to the cited text or mark verification as unavailable.
- Format: do not format unstable content as if final.
- Submission: verify current official requirements and access date.
