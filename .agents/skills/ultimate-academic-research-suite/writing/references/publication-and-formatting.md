## Venue selection

Assess scope, audience, article type, methodological fit, length, review model, indexing, fees, timelines, data/code policies, ethics, and reputation. Use official sources for current facts. Do not rely on a journal title or impact metric alone.

Screen warning signs: unverifiable editorial board, deceptive indexing claims, hidden fees, guaranteed acceptance, implausible review speed, copied website content, or unclear ownership.

## Open access and identifiers

Verify current funder and institution requirements before choosing gold, hybrid, green, or other routes. Check embargo, license, repository, and accepted-manuscript rules.

Use persistent identifiers where relevant:

- ORCID for authors.
- DOI or other stable identifier for outputs.
- ROR for institutions.
- Registry/preregistration identifiers for studies.

Never fabricate an identifier.

## Formatting

Treat the venue template as authoritative. Normalize:

- Heading hierarchy.
- Page/word limits.
- Margins, type, spacing, and numbering.
- Citation and reference style.
- Equations, units, abbreviations, and nomenclature.
- Table/figure placement and captions.
- Appendices and supplements.
- Anonymization for review.

Keep content and presentation changes separable in the revision record.

## Figures and tables

Verify every value against source data. Use accessible color, readable labels, units, uncertainty, sample sizes, and explanatory notes. Ensure citations and permissions for reused material.

## Submission package

As required, prepare:

- Manuscript and title page.
- Cover letter.
- Highlights, graphical abstract, or lay summary.
- Authorship/CRediT, funding, conflicts, ethics, consent, and acknowledgments.
- Data/code/materials availability.
- AI-use disclosure.
- Suggested/excluded reviewers with legitimate rationale.
- Supplementary files and reporting checklists.
- Response-to-reviewers package for revisions.

## Rendering and final checks

Render the actual deliverable and inspect:

1. Missing glyphs, overflow, clipped text, and broken page breaks.
2. Table and figure legibility.
3. Cross-references, numbering, citations, URLs, and bookmarks.
4. Headers, footers, anonymity, and metadata.
5. Consistency between manuscript and supplements.

Do not declare submission-ready until current official requirements and the rendered package have been checked.
