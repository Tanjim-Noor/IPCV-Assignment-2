## Citation audit

For each in-text citation verify:

1. The source exists and metadata matches.
2. The cited passage supports the nearby claim.
3. The source type is adequate for the claim.
4. Page/section details are present for quotations where required.
5. The in-text citation and reference entry correspond.
6. The target style is applied consistently.

For each reference-list entry verify that it is cited or intentionally listed under venue rules. Never infer a DOI, issue, page range, author order, or title from partial data.

## Claim verification

Classify important claims:

- `verified`: directly supported by inspected evidence.
- `partially-supported`: source supports only part or weaker scope.
- `contradicted`: credible evidence conflicts.
- `unverifiable`: source unavailable or insufficient.
- `author-assertion`: supplied by the author without independent evidence.
- `proposal`: future action, not completed work.

Trace quantitative claims, quotations, legal/policy requirements, novelty claims, and causal statements with particular care.

## Data and methods integrity

Check for:

- Fabricated or impossible data.
- Selective omission inconsistent with the stated protocol.
- Undisclosed exclusions, transformations, or analytic flexibility.
- Leakage between training and evaluation.
- Inappropriate statistical tests or causal language.
- Results inconsistent across prose, tables, figures, and supplements.
- Methods described as completed when only planned.

Do not invent a missing method detail. Ask, mark it, or narrow the claim.

## Originality

Compare text and ideas with sources. Distinguish common academic phrases from distinctive expression or intellectual contribution. Phrasebank use does not remove the need to cite ideas and evidence.

Treat automated plagiarism or AI-text detector output as weak screening evidence, not proof. Use source comparison, citation tracing, version history, and authorship records.

## Authorship and disclosure

Use the target venue and funder rules. Record contributor roles with an appropriate taxonomy such as CRediT when requested. Do not list an AI system as an author. Disclose AI assistance where required, stating tool, purpose, scope, and human verification without claiming capabilities not used.

Verify current rules before drafting a final disclosure.

## Ethics and reproducibility

Confirm, where applicable:

- Ethics/IRB approval or exemption.
- Consent and privacy protection.
- Sensitive-data handling.
- Conflicts of interest.
- Funding and sponsor role.
- Data, code, materials, and preregistration availability.
- Corrections or deviations from protocol.

Block `verified` and `submission-ready` status when a required ethical approval, consent, source, or core result cannot be substantiated.
